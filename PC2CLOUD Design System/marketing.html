Why PC2CLOUD

Cloud storage shouldn't cost you every month.

You already own the hardware. We provide the software that turns it into a private cloud — accessible from anywhere, encrypted end-to-end, and yours to keep.

Unlimited, free storage

Add another drive, plug it in, watch your capacity grow. There are no caps, no upgrade prompts, no monthly fees.

Your data, end-to-end

Files transfer over encrypted WebRTC peer-to-peer connections. We never store them on our servers — only signaling metadata.

Direct P2P transfers

No bandwidth bottlenecks. Your devices connect directly when possible, with relay fallback for stubborn networks.

Multi-device sync

Access from anywhere

Architecture

Files stay on your PC.
Always.

Our servers handle authentication, device registration, and signaling — nothing else. When your devices talk to each other, they connect directly over encrypted WebRTC. Even we can't see your data.

Signaling only. Server brokers connections, then steps aside.
Direct P2P transfers. WebRTC data channels, encrypted.
Relay fallback. Symmetric NATs use our TURN servers — still encrypted.
Zero-knowledge metadata. We store file names, not file contents.

FAQ

Common questions.

Does my PC need to stay on?

Yes — your PC is the storage server. When it's off, your files aren't reachable. Most users leave a dedicated PC running 24/7, or run PC2CLOUD on a machine they use daily (it idles at near-zero CPU when nothing's transferring).

What happens if my hard drive fails?

PC2CLOUD doesn't replicate files automatically (yet). Treat it like a single hard drive: back up anything irreplaceable separately. Pro plans include cloud backup snapshots as a safety net.

How is this different from a Synology NAS?

A NAS is dedicated hardware you buy. PC2CLOUD uses a PC you already have. Same idea — your storage, your control — just no new hardware purchase.

Is the source code available?

Yes. The full monorepo (Next.js dashboard, Electron client, Express signaling server) is open source on GitHub.

macOS and Linux support?

Coming. Windows ships first because it's the most common "spare PC" platform. macOS is next.
